## When Is Tooth Extraction Needed?

- Severe tooth decay that cannot be restored with a filling or crown
- Advanced gum disease causing loose teeth
- Impacted wisdom teeth causing pain or infection
- Overcrowding requiring orthodontic treatment
- Fractured or broken teeth beyond repair

## Simple vs Surgical Extraction

**Simple extraction** is performed on visible teeth using local anesthesia. The dentist loosens the tooth and removes it with forceps.

**Surgical extraction** is needed for impacted or broken teeth. The dentist makes a small incision in the gum to access and remove the tooth.

## The Extraction Process at Apollo

1. **Examination and X-ray:** Assess the tooth and surrounding bone
2. **Local anesthesia:** Complete numbness of the area
3. **Extraction:** Gentle removal with minimal trauma
4. **Post-operative care:** Detailed instructions for healing

## Recovery Tips After Tooth Extraction

- Bite on gauze for 30-45 minutes to control bleeding
- Apply ice packs to reduce swelling (first 24 hours)
- Avoid hot foods, spicy foods, and straws for 48 hours
- Do not smoke or use tobacco for at least 72 hours
- Eat soft foods like yogurt, soup, and mashed potatoes
- Rinse gently with warm salt water after 24 hours
